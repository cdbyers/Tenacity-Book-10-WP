B6.5 Language focus: The past simple passive
We use passive verbs when who did the action is obvious or is not important, when we want to focus
on what happened, not who did it. We use the past participles of verbs to make the passive tense.
Example: 2,000 cars were exported in 1999.
1
Write these sentences in your exercise book with the correct form of the verb.
Example:
The government builds 100 schools every year.
More schools were built by the government in 2005 than in 2004.

3 Many experiments carry out / are carried out in laboratories.
4 It is important that scientists record / are recorded their observations accurately.
5 The student filled / is filled the test tube.
6 Many important discoveries made / are made by accident.
