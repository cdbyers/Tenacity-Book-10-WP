A7.6 Language focus: Prepositions of time
We talk about when things happen using prepositions of time. We use:
●
The preposition in for months, years, centuries, and long periods.
Examples:
Months in July
Years in 1960
Centuries in the 19
th
century
Long periods in the mornings
Seasons in summer
●
The preposition at for a precise time.
Examples:
A precise time at 7.00 a.m.
The weekend at the weekend
Night at night
Festive periods at Christmas
●
The preposition on for days and dates.
Examples:
Special days on my birthday
Days of the week on Tuesday
Dates on the 24
th
of December
●
Exceptions: in the past
at present
in the future
There is NO preposition of time if the day / year has each, every, last, next, this before it.
Examples: each year
every Monday
last week
next day
this week
