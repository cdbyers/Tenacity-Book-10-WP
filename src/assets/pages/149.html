When we meet a new word (a word that we haven’t seen before) in a text, it is not always
necessary to stop and look up the word in a dictionary or ask someone what it means.
Reading can become boring if we have to keep stopping. Sometimes we can just carry on
reading and not worry about a few words that we don’t understand. Sometimes though, the
meaning of the word can be guessed from how it is used in the text, its context.
Read the passage again, write down any unfamiliar words and work out their possible
meanings from the context. Check your partner’s definitions in a dictionary then work in
pairs to match words to their definitions by means of a quiz.
